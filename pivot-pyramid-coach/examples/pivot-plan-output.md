# Example Pivot Planning Document

A sample pivot planning document for a Problem Pivot.

---

## PIVOT PLANNING DOCUMENT

**Date**: December 2024
**Company**: CartRecovery (fictional example)
**Author**: Founder/CEO
**Status**: Under Review

---

## Executive Summary

We are pivoting our Problem layer from "abandoned cart recovery" to "reducing wasted ad spend." Evidence shows that our current problem isn't urgent enough to drive adoption, while our $1M+ customer segment has strong pain around ad spend efficiency. This is a Problem Pivot that will cascade to Solution, Technology, and Growth layers.

---

## Current State

| Layer | Current Hypothesis |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Customers** | Small e-commerce stores on Shopify ($100K-$2M revenue) |
| **Problem** | They lose revenue from abandoned carts |
| **Solution** | AI-personalized cart recovery emails with dynamic discounts |
| **Technology** | Shopify integration + GPT-based personalization |
| **Growth** | Shopify App Store, content marketing, cold outreach |

### Evidence Summary (Current State)

| Layer | Evidence Level | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| Customers | Quantified | 150 customers, but only 30% ($1M+) have good retention |
| Problem | Anecdotal | Customers acknowledge but don't prioritize |
| Solution | Anecdotal | Only 20% better than Klaviyoâ€”not enough differentiation |
| Technology | Pattern | Integration works, no major issues |
| Growth | Anecdotal | Multiple channels tried, none achieving CAC < LTV |

---

## Target State

| Layer | New Hypothesis |
|-------|---------------|
| **Customers** | Shopify stores with $1M-5M revenue (focused segment) |
| **Problem** | They waste 30%+ of ad spend on ineffective campaigns |
| **Solution** | AI-powered ad spend optimizer that identifies waste and reallocates budget |
| **Technology** | Google/Meta ad API integrations + ML models for spend optimization |
| **Growth** | Shopify App Store + "Free ad spend audit" content marketing |

---

## Cascade Analysis

**Primary Pivot**: Problem Layer

We're changing from:
> "E-commerce stores struggle with abandoned carts"

To:
> "E-commerce stores waste 30%+ of ad spend on ineffective campaigns"

### Required Changes

**Solution Layer** (Must change)
- **Old**: Cart recovery emails with AI personalization
- **New**: Ad spend optimization platform with waste identification
- **Effort**: 3-4 months of product development
- **Reason**: New problem requires fundamentally different solution

**Technology Layer** (Must change)
- **Old**: Shopify integration + email sending
- **New**: Google Ads API, Meta Ads API, ML models for spend analysis
- **Effort**: 2-3 months of engineering
- **Reason**: Different solution requires different technical capabilities

**Growth Layer** (Must change)
- **Old**: "Recover lost revenue from abandoned carts"
- **New**: "Stop wasting 30% of your ad budget"
- **Effort**: 2-4 weeks to revamp messaging and landing pages
- **Reason**: New value proposition needs new positioning

### What We're Keeping

**Customer Layer** (Stable with focus)
- Narrowing from $100K-$2M to $1M-5M
- We have evidence this segment retains better
- They have budget for ad optimization tools
- No cascade impact from this focus

---

## Evidence Base

Why we believe this pivot will work:

### Customer Segment Validation
- Our $1M+ customers have 3x better retention than smaller stores (Quantified)
- 45 customer interviews confirmed ad spend is top-3 priority (Pattern)
- Average $1M-5M store spends $5-20K/month on ads (Quantified)

### Problem Urgency Signals
- 18 of 20 customers mentioned ad spend frustration unprompted (Pattern)
- 12 customers showed us their ad dashboards (Willingness test passed)
- 8 customers said they'd pay for a tool that reduced waste (Pattern)
- Average customer estimates 25-40% of spend is wasted (Anecdotal)

### Solution Differentiation
- No dominant "ad waste identifier" for SMB e-commerce (Market gap)
- Enterprise tools (Adobe, Sprinklr) cost $10K+/month (Price gap)
- Our ML team has experience in ad optimization (Capability)

### Leading Indicators (Measure Weekly)

| Metric | Current | Target | Threshold |

| Pilot signups | 0 | 20 | 10 |
| Time to value (first insight) | N/A | <1 day | <3 days |

### Lagging Indicators (Measure at 90 Days)

| Metric | Current | Target | Threshold |
|--------|---------|--------|-----------|
| Paying customers | 0 | 30 | 15 |
| MRR | $0 | $15K | $7.5K |
| Retention (Month 1) | N/A | 90%+ | 80% |
| CAC | N/A | <$500 | <$800 |

### Kill Criteria

We will abandon this pivot if:
- After 60 days, <5 pilot signups
- After 90 days, <$5K MRR
- Retention falls below 70% in first month
- 3+ customers report no value from insights

## Communication Plan

### Internal (Team)

**Announcement**: All-hands meeting Week 1
**Key messages**:
- Why we're pivoting (evidence from customer conversations)
- What specifically is changing
- What stays the same (customer focus, team, mission)
- Timeline and milestones
- How each role is affected

**Ongoing**: Weekly pivot standup to track progress

### Customers

**Existing customers**:
- Continue supporting cart recovery product
- Offer early access to new product
- Don't force migration

**Prospects**:
- Update website and app store listing
- New messaging: "Stop wasting 30% of your ad budget"
- New content: "Free ad spend audit"

### Investors

**When**: Before public announcement
**Key messages**:
- Evidence-based decision (share customer interview findings)
- Larger market opportunity (ad spend > cart recovery)
- Clear plan with success criteria
- Request: No additional capital needed for validation phase
